My hometown lies at the confluence of several rivers, which then flow into Corral Bay and the Pacific Ocean. It rains a lot there (really, a lot), and that climate gives life to a unique ecosystem in South America: the Valdivian temperate rainforest. The region is also known for its wetlands, some of which were formed after the land subsidence and flooding caused by the 1960 earthquake, the largest ever recorded. To me, it is one of the most beautiful places in the world.
